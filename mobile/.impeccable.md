## Design Context

### Users
Individuals and couples managing a shared household budget. They open the app at the end of the day or during a quiet moment — not in a rush, not in crisis. The job: check in on the household's financial health, log what was spent, feel confident things are on track. Context is reflective, not reactive.

### Brand Personality
**Japanese philosophy of mindful stewardship.** This is the core identity — not a hint, not decoration. Ebisu (Japanese god of fortune), 福 (fortune/happiness), "vessels", "steady obligations", "tending to" — the language and visual identity deliberately evoke the idea that money is something to be stewarded with intention and care, not tracked with anxiety.

Three words: **Calm. Intentional. Warm.**

The app should feel like a trusted ritual — opening it is like checking in on a garden, not auditing a spreadsheet.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Light mode only.** Warm backgrounds (rice/blush palette), never clinical white.
- **Japanese-inflected without being costume.** The 福 glyph, AoboshiOne display font, poetic microcopy — these should feel natural to the brand, not cosplay.
- **Contrast through darkness.** The dark navy budget card (#20243A) with gold rule accents is the right instinct — one rich dark surface against warm light backgrounds creates depth without going full dark mode.
- **The mascot (Ebisu character) is a design asset**, not an afterthought. It carries emotional warmth and should be treated with the same care as any brand element.
- **Anti-references**: Not YNAB (too utilitarian), not Mint (too aggressive), not any "fintech" aesthetic (cold, chart-heavy, blue-everything). Nothing that looks like a dashboard.
- **Motion**: Already well-considered — staggered entrances, purposeful easing. Reinforce the calm rhythm, never add urgency through motion.

### Design Principles
1. **Stillness as a feature.** The interface should never feel busy. Whitespace is not emptiness — it's composure.
2. **Language is design.** Microcopy like "what shall we tend to?" is part of the visual identity. Words and UI are one system.
3. **Warmth through contrast.** The dark budget card against warm surfaces, gold against navy — use deliberate contrast to create richness, not decoration for its own sake.
4. **The mascot earns its place.** Ebisu the character should appear where it adds emotional presence, not as filler. His expressions and poses are communication.
5. **Never feel like a finance app.** If a screen could belong to Mint or YNAB, it doesn't belong here.
